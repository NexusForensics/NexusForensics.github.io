Refund Policy

This policy explains how refund requests, cancellations, technical issues, and Vendor on Record processed payments are handled for Nexus Forensics software.

Last updated: June 18, 2026

1. Overview

Nexus Forensics wants customers to have a clear path for billing questions, cancellations, and refund requests. This policy applies unless a signed agreement with Nexus Forensics Inc. states different refund terms.

2. Vendor on Record processed purchases

Some purchases may be processed by a Vendor on Record. If your receipt or checkout says a Vendor on Record processed the transaction, refund requests may be handled through that vendor's support and transaction systems. The applicable refund process may also be described in the Vendor on Record's own refund policy.

3. How to request a refund

To request a refund, contact sales@nexus-forensics.ca with the purchaser name, email address, order number if available, product purchased, date of purchase, and reason for the request. For Vendor on Record transactions, you may also use the receipt, manage subscription, or refund request links provided by the Vendor on Record.

4. 14-day refund window

Refund requests made within 14 days of the initial purchase are eligible for review and may be approved, subject to applicable law, the Vendor on Record's policies, and evidence of misuse, abuse, or violation of the Terms and Conditions.

After 14 days, refund requests are reviewed on a case-by-case basis. Factors may include whether the software was activated or used, the length of use, the nature of the issue, support history, subscription status, and any legal rights that apply.

5. Transaction fees and extended use

Where permitted by law and the Vendor on Record's processing rules, approved discretionary refunds after the 14-day window may exclude non-recoverable transaction, platform, or payment processing costs. These costs may be up to 5% of the transaction amount.

Use of the software beyond 14 days, including use on day 15 or later, may also be considered when determining whether a refund is approved or whether any non-refundable portion applies. This does not limit any mandatory refund, cancellation, or withdrawal rights you may have under applicable law.

6. Technical or product issues

If a technical issue or material product defect prevents you from using the purchased product as described, contact Nexus Forensics first so we can attempt to resolve the issue. Because Nexus Forensics products are designed for local-first evidence workflows, troubleshooting may require information about your software version, license status, operating environment, and error messages, but Nexus Forensics does not require custody of your evidence files to review a billing or refund request.

If the issue cannot be resolved within a reasonable time, a refund may be approved where appropriate or required by law.

7. Subscriptions and cancellations

You may cancel a subscription to prevent future renewal charges. Cancellation normally takes effect at the end of the current paid billing period unless otherwise stated in checkout, Vendor on Record subscription management, or a written agreement. Access may continue until the end of the paid term.

8. Non-refundable situations

Refunds may be declined for completed billing periods, failure to cancel before renewal, misuse of the software, violation of the Terms and Conditions, or repeated refund abuse. This does not limit any non-waivable rights you may have under applicable consumer protection law.

9. Processing approved refunds

Approved refunds are usually returned to the original payment method where possible. Timing may depend on the Vendor on Record, the payment method, banks, card networks, and applicable law.

10. Statutory rights

Nothing in this policy limits mandatory refund, cancellation, or withdrawal rights that apply under local consumer protection laws.
