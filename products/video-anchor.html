Video Evidence Anchoring

The Video Anchor

A Nexus Forensics product for turning long video sources into anchored evidence timelines with time-coded findings, clips, still frames, notes, transcripts, hashes, and review-ready packages.

See workflow Evidence outputs Contact sales

What It Does

Find the moment, preserve the context, package the proof.

Video evidence often lives inside a larger recording. The Video Anchor keeps each important moment tied to its source, timestamp, surrounding context, extracted media, and investigator notes.

Anchor moments

Mark key timestamps with notes, labels, confidence, source context, and review status.

Extract evidence

Create still frames, clips, thumbnails, transcript snippets, and local media references.

Preserve source

Keep original URLs, file paths, capture times, hashes, and chain-of-review notes connected.

Export timeline

Build a readable report that shows the video timeline, anchored findings, media, and hash manifest.

Workflow

A review path for video that is too long to explain from memory.

The product is shaped around repeated review: ingest, inspect, anchor, extract, and hand off.

Step 1Add source

Open a public video URL or local case media file.

Step 2Review

Move through the recording with frame-aware context.

Step 3Anchor

Pin the exact timestamps that matter to the investigation.

Step 4Extract

Save clips, stills, notes, transcript snippets, and hashes.

Step 5Report

Export a time-coded evidence package for review or disclosure.

Evidence Outputs

Every anchored moment should be easy to revisit.

The Video Anchor is designed to keep an important second of video connected to the original source and the surrounding evidence. Reviewers should see the timestamp, still, clip, note, source, hash, and case context without hunting through folders.
